Maintenance

Every resident and ocupant is responsible for maintaining their unit in clean, hygienic conditions and performing basic maintenance. Proper care prevents damage and ensures a comfortable living environment. Todo residente y ocupante es responsable de mantener su unidad en condiciones limpias e higiénicas y realizar mantenimiento básico. El cuidado adecuado previene daños y asegura un ambiente de vida confortable.

Maintenance Guidelines
Countertops & Cabinets

Proper cleaning methods for different surfaces in your unit.

Recommended Products:

• Vinyl floors: Mild pH-neutral cleaner or vinegar solution (1 cup vinegar to 1 gallon water)
• Countertops: Manufacturer-recommended cleaner or mild soap and water
• Bathroom surfaces: Non-abrasive cleaners with anti-mold properties
• Windows: Glass cleaner or vinegar solution
• Carpets: Mild carpet shampoo or baking soda

What NOT to Do:

• Don't use bleach or ammonia on vinyl floors
• Avoid abrasive scrubbers on countertops
• Never pour harsh chemicals down drains
• Don't let spills sit for long periods
• Don't use wax-based products on vinyl floors
• Avoid steam cleaning vinyl floors

Maintenance Tips:

• Clean spills immediately to prevent stains
• Use furniture pads to protect floors
• Ventilate bathrooms after showering
• Regularly clean baseboards and corners
• Sweep or vacuum floors before mopping
• Use doormats to reduce dirt accumulation

Cabinets

Proper care for kitchen cabinets and storage.

Recommended Products:

• Wood cabinets: Mild soap and water
• Painted cabinets: Non-abrasive cleaner
• Hardware: Mild detergent and soft cloth

What NOT to Do:

• Don't use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads
• Avoid excessive water on wood surfaces
• Never hang wet towels on cabinet doors
• Don't overload shelves

Maintenance Tips:

• Wipe spills immediately
• Clean handles regularly
• Adjust hinges if doors sag
• Organize contents for easy access

Appliances
Refrigerator

Set temperature to 37°F (3°C) for refrigerator and 0°F (-18°C) for freezer.

How to Use:

• Keep food properly covered
• Allow hot food to cool before storing

What NOT to Do:

Maintenance:

A/C Room
Air Conditioner

Keep the temperature between 72-76°F (22-24°C) for comfort and efficiency.

How to Use:

• Keep doors and windows closed when the A/C is on
• Clean or check filters monthly
• Adjust the temperature gradually
• Use auto mode to save energy
• Allow air to circulate freely around vents

What NOT to Do:

• Do not set the thermostat to extreme temperatures
• Do not cover air vents
• Do not turn the A/C on and off repeatedly
• Do not attempt to repair the unit yourself
• Do not block airflow with furniture or objects

Maintenance:

• A/C won't turn on: Check that the breaker in the electrical panel is ON
• Not cooling enough: Check and clean the filter, make sure doors and windows are closed
• Water leaking from A/C: Check that the drain is not clogged
• Making noise: Turn off the unit and report to maintenance
• Remote not responding: Make sure the thermostat is on and the breaker is ON

Thermostat

Set the thermostat to 72-76°F (22-24°C) for comfort and energy savings.

How to Use:

• Use auto mode to regulate temperature
• Adjust the temperature gradually, not all at once
• Keep the area around the thermostat clear
• If leaving home for several hours, raise the temperature to save energy

What NOT to Do:

• Do not set the thermostat below 67°F (19°C)
• Do not turn off the thermostat completely
• Do not try to open or repair the thermostat
• Do not cover the thermostat with furniture, curtains, or decorations

Maintenance:

• Keep the thermostat clean and dust-free
• Check periodically that the display is on
• This thermostat does NOT use batteries, it works with electrical power
• If the thermostat does not turn on, check the breaker in the electrical panel and make sure it is ON
• If the problem persists, report it to maintenance

Proper Use of Water Heater
Water Heater

How to Use:

• Always use hot water with caution to avoid burns.
• Keep the area around the water heater clear.
• Open the cold water first, then the hot water, and adjust to your preference.

What NOT to Do:

• Do not overload shelves near the heater.
• Avoid placing liquids without lids near the heater.
• Never use sharp objects to remove ice or debris.
• Do not store hot food directly inside (not applicable for water heaters).

Maintenance:

• If the water is not heating, check the switch next to the heater and the breaker in the electrical panel to ensure they are ON.
• If any of the above are OFF, return them to their original ON position.

After verifying everything is ON, wait 20 minutes and check if you have hot water.

Laundry Room
Washing Machine

Use only high-efficiency detergent and distribute the load evenly for best performance.

How to Use:

• Separate clothes by color and fabric type
• Measure detergent according to load size
• Leave the door open after use to prevent mold
• Run the cleaning cycle monthly
• Do not exceed the maximum load line
• Use laundry bags for delicate items
• Check pockets before washing
• Select the appropriate cycle for the type of clothes
• Wait for the cycle to finish before opening the door

What NOT to Do:

• Do not overload the machine
• Avoid using regular detergent (use only HE)
• Never wash garments with loose buttons or open zippers
• Do not force the door open during a cycle
• Do not slam or force the door
• Do not move the washer from its place
• Do not put rugs, shoes, or heavy items inside
• Do not use hot water for delicate clothes
• Do not leave metal objects inside the drum

Maintenance:

• If the cycle does not start, check that the breaker in the electrical panel is not off
• If the washer does not start, make sure the door is properly closed
• Clean the lint filter and detergent dispenser monthly
• If there is a bad odor, run a cleaning cycle with vinegar or special products
• Check that the drain hose is not clogged
• If the washer vibrates excessively, make sure it is level

Dryer

Clean the lint filter before each use, do not overload to avoid wrinkles.

How to Use:

• Clean the lint filter after every load
• Use the proper heat setting for each fabric type
• Remove clothes promptly to reduce wrinkles
• Do not mix heavy and light clothes
• Check pockets before drying
• Shake out clothes before placing in the dryer
• Use dryer balls to improve drying and reduce static
• Ensure proper ventilation

What NOT to Do:

• Under no circumstances remove the filter when drying clothes
• Do not dry rubber, plastic, or foam items
• Avoid overloading the drum
• Never ignore lint buildup
• Do not put excessively wet clothes inside (wring them out first)
• Do not use the dryer if you hear strange noises
• Do not cover the dryer vent
• Do not use liquid fabric softeners inside the dryer

Maintenance:

• If the dryer does not turn on, check that the breaker is ON in the electrical panel
• If clothes come out damp, check that there is not too much laundry in the drum
• If the dryer makes strange noises, stop it and check for loose objects
• If you smell burning, turn off the dryer and clean the filter and duct immediately
• Make sure the door closes properly before starting the cycle

Cuarto de A/C
Aire Acondicionado

Mantenga la temperatura entre 72-76°F (22-24°C) para comodidad y eficiencia.

Cómo Usar:

• Mantenga puertas y ventanas cerradas cuando el A/C esté encendido
• Limpie o revise los filtros mensualmente
• Ajuste la temperatura gradualmente
• Use el modo automático para ahorrar energía
• Permita que el aire circule sin obstrucciones alrededor de las rejillas

Qué NO hacer:

• No ajuste el termostato a temperaturas extremas
• No cubra las rejillas de ventilación
• No apague y encienda el A/C repetidamente
• No intente reparar el equipo usted mismo
• No bloquee el flujo de aire con muebles u objetos

Mantenimiento:

• El A/C no enciende: Verifique que el breaker en el panel eléctrico esté en ON
• No enfría lo suficiente: Revise y limpie el filtro, asegúrese de que puertas y ventanas estén cerradas
• Sale agua del A/C: Revise que el drenaje no esté obstruido
• Hace ruido: Apague el equipo y repórtelo a mantenimiento
• El control no responde: Asegúrese de que el termostato esté encendido y el breaker en ON

Termostato

Ajuste el termostato a 72-76°F (22-24°C) para mayor confort y ahorro de energía.

Cómo Usar:

• Use el modo automático para regular la temperatura
• Ajuste la temperatura poco a poco, no de golpe
• Mantenga despejada el área alrededor del termostato
• Si sale de casa por varias horas, suba la temperatura para ahorrar energía

Qué NO hacer:

• No ajuste el termostato por debajo de 67°F (19°C)
• No apague el termostato completamente
• No intente abrir o reparar el termostato
• No cubra el termostato con muebles, cortinas o decoraciones

Mantenimiento:

• Mantenga el termostato limpio y libre de polvo
• Revise periódicamente que la pantalla esté encendida
• Este termostato NO usa baterías, funciona con energía eléctrica
• Si el termostato no enciende, verifique el breaker en el panel eléctrico y asegúrese que esté en ON
• Si el problema persiste, repórtelo a mantenimiento

Uso Correcto del Calentador de Agua
Calentador de Agua

Cómo Usar:

• Utilice siempre el agua caliente con precaución para evitar quemaduras.
• Mantenga el área alrededor del calentador despejada.
• Abra primero el agua fría, luego la caliente y ajuste a su preferencia.

Qué NO hacer:

• No sobrecargue los estantes cerca del calentador.
• Evite colocar líquidos sin tapa cerca del calentador.
• Nunca use objetos filosos para remover hielo o residuos.
• No almacene comida caliente dentro (no aplica para calentadores de agua).

Mantenimiento:

• Si el agua no calienta, revise el interruptor junto al calentador y el breaker en el panel eléctrico para asegurarse de que estén en ON.
• Si alguno de los anteriores está en OFF, póngalo en su posición original ON.

Después de verificar que todo está en ON, espere 20 minutos y revise si ya tiene agua caliente.

Áreas Generales
Cuidado del Piso de Vinilo

Para mantener el piso de vinilo limpio y en buen estado:

  • Barra o aspire regularmente
  • Limpie con trapeador húmedo y detergente neutro
  • Seque cualquier derrame de inmediato
  • Use protectores en las patas de los muebles
  • Coloque tapetes en las entradas

Evite: usar líquidos corrosivos, cloro, amoníaco, limpiadores abrasivos o vapor. No deje agua estancada ni arrastre muebles pesados sin protección.

Cuidado y Limpieza de Ventanas de Cristal

Para mantener las ventanas limpias y funcionales:

  • Limpie el vidrio con limpiavidrios o mezcla de vinagre y agua
  • Use paño suave o papel sin pelusa
  • Limpie marcos y rieles mensualmente
  • Revise que los seguros funcionen bien

Evite: objetos filosos, esponjas abrasivas, líquidos corrosivos o forzar las ventanas.

Cuidado de Puertas (Entrada y Habitaciones)

Para conservar las puertas limpias y en buen funcionamiento:

  • Limpie con paño húmedo y jabón suave
  • Revise bisagras y manijas periódicamente
  • Lubrique bisagras si hacen ruido
  • Mantenga libres de obstáculos las áreas cercanas

Evite: líquidos corrosivos, limpiadores abrasivos, colgar objetos pesados o cerrar las puertas con fuerza.

Guía General de Buenas Prácticas
  • No vierta líquidos corrosivos, solventes, ácidos o químicos fuertes en ninguna superficie, drenaje, electrodoméstico o accesorio
